## Detailed Summary

Analysis date: 2026-04-30 in Asia/Tokyo. U.S. market date: 2026-04-30, using the 2026-04-29 U.S. close as the latest completed session. Today's market is a second consecutive macro-and-earnings event window: Q1 GDP, March personal income/outlays and PCE, weekly jobless claims, the ECB decision, and Apple earnings all sit on top of the prior day's Fed decision and Big Tech earnings.

The tradeable regime is mixed, event-waiting, and quality-led. The Fed held the target range at 3.50%-3.75% on 2026-04-29, but the statement cited elevated inflation partly tied to global energy prices and the vote had four dissents, including one cut dissent and three officials opposed to retaining an easing bias ([Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm)). That means the market cannot treat rate cuts as automatic. The immediate chain is: GDP/PCE/jobless claims -> 2Y yield and DXY -> QQQ / IWM breadth -> BTC and high-beta equities.

The 2026-04-29 close was resilient but narrow: S&P 500 slipped less than 0.1% to 7,135.95, Dow fell 0.6%, Nasdaq rose less than 0.1%, and Russell 2000 fell 0.6% as oil and Treasury yields rose ([AP](https://apnews.com/article/f01ef6ed118e30e2cde9d25b4a1fdfc5)). Big Tech earnings validate AI/cloud demand, but the capex debate is now the key equity risk. Microsoft reported $82.9B revenue and Azure +40% ([Microsoft](https://news.microsoft.com/source/2026/04/29/microsoft-cloud-and-ai-strength-fuels-third-quarter-results/)); Amazon reported $181.5B net sales and AWS +28% ([Amazon IR](https://ir.aboutamazon.com/news-release/news-release-details/2026/Amazon-com-Announces-First-Quarter-Results/default.aspx)); Meta reported $56.3B revenue but raised 2026 capex guidance to $125B-$145B ([Meta IR](https://investor.atmeta.com/investor-news/press-release-details/2026/Meta-Reports-First-Quarter-2026-Results/default.aspx)); Alphabet's cloud strength is the cleanest positive read-through, with reports citing Google Cloud +63% ([The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/tech/920815/google-alphabet-q1-2026-earnings-sundar-pichai)).

My trading judgment for today and the next 1-3 U.S. sessions: buy quality only when rates and DXY do not fight the tape. If 2Y yields and DXY fall after the 8:30 a.m. ET data, QQQ, SMH, GOOGL, MSFT, AMZN, NVDA, and BTC-linked beta can rally. If oil stays hot and yields/DXY rise, favor XLE, OIH, GLD, GDX, quality cash-flow names, and avoid IWM / weak high-beta.
