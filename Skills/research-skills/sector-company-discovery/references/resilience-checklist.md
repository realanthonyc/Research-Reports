# Resilience Checklist

Use this when the user wants the most resilient, defensive, or survivable company in a group.

## Core Questions

1. Can the company stay profitable or cash-generative through a downturn?
2. Does the balance sheet allow it to survive without forced dilution or distressed refinancing?
3. Is demand recurring, mission-critical, or likely to recover quickly?
4. Does the company have pricing power or cost flexibility?
5. Is management historically disciplined with capital allocation?

## Signs Of High Resilience

- net cash or modest leverage
- stable or recurring revenue
- high gross margin or flexible cost structure
- essential product or service
- diversified customers
- strong free cash flow conversion

## Signs Of Weak Resilience

- heavy leverage with near-term maturities
- one-customer or one-product dependence
- structurally weak margins
- demand tied to discretionary spending or capital markets windows
- repeated equity issuance to fund operations

## Output Rule

If resilience is the main user goal, make it one of the top 2 ranking dimensions rather than a side note.
