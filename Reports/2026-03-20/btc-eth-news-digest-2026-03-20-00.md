## Detailed Summary

The key development in the last 24 hours is that BTC and ETH were hit by a double macro shock rather than a crypto-native shock. First, the March 18 FOMC reaffirmed that the Fed is not in a hurry to ease, with the policy rate kept at `3.5%-3.75%` and the updated 2026 `PCE` and `core PCE` medians both at `2.7%`. Second, on March 19 Brent briefly rose above `US$119/bbl` before easing, which pushed the market to price more inflation risk and fewer near-term cuts. For crypto, that is the wrong combination: tighter effective liquidity, firmer real yields, and a stronger incentive to reduce high-beta exposure. [Federal Reserve SEP](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcprojtabl20260318.htm) [AP oil shock](https://apnews.com/article/1abeddf7c4bf19d1dc96b3f23c1de402)

The market reaction confirms this read. At the time of writing, CoinMarketCap showed `BTC` at `US$69,849.15`, down `1.89%` over 24 hours with a range of `US$68,805.52` to `US$71,598.85`. `ETH` was `US$2,133.59`, down `3.13%` with a range of `US$2,099.89` to `US$2,232.20`. Barron’s separately reported Bitcoin falling below `US$70,000` on March 19 and Ethereum down `6.8%`, which is directionally consistent with the same macro stress. BTC is still holding the cleaner structure than ETH because it absorbs macro risk as the higher-quality crypto beta and quasi-digital-gold expression, while ETH behaves more like higher-beta tech liquidity. [CoinMarketCap BTC](https://coinmarketcap.com/currencies/bitcoin/) [CoinMarketCap ETH](https://coinmarketcap.com/currencies/ethereum/) [Barron's crypto](https://www.barrons.com/articles/bitcoin-price-xrp-ethereum-fed-powell-oil-033bcaee)

This is not a uniformly bearish crypto setup. IBD reported that spot bitcoin ETFs took in `US$58.37m` on the latest Thursday flow and nearly `US$587m` for that week, while BTC had recently traded as high as `US$73,927` before rolling over. That means institutional demand still exists, but the last 24-hour move showed clearly that macro pressure can still dominate those flows in the short run. In other words, the right desk framing is `selective, not euphoric`, and `prefer BTC over ETH unless macro conditions improve`. [IBD](https://www.investors.com/news/bitcoin-price-march-73k-high-etf-flows-terawulf-wulf-stock-bitcoin-miners-crypto-stocks/)

My judgment for the next one to three sessions is that BTC remains tactically stronger than ETH, but neither asset gets a clean upside until oil retreats further or front-end yields roll over. The macro read is bearish for absolute crypto beta, but only mildly so for BTC relative to ETH. If rates and the dollar stop tightening, BTC can stabilize first. If the oil/inflation channel stays live, ETH is more likely to keep lagging.
