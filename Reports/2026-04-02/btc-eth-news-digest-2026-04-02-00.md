## Detailed Summary

The latest BTC/ETH signal is not about a major crypto-native catalyst. It is about whether crypto can hold up better than equities and weaker macro-beta assets while the market flips from "soft labor" to "oil-shock stagflation" within the same 24 hours. On April 1, ADP showed only 62,000 private payroll gains, which by itself would normally help crypto through lower-yield expectations. But ISM manufacturing also showed prices paid jumping to 78.3, and the later Trump speech on Iran pushed oil sharply higher and sent Asian risk assets lower. Sources: [ADP](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/adp-national-employment-report-private-sector-employment-increased-by-62-000-jobs-in-march-annual-pay-was-up-4-5-302731281.html), [ISM / Reuters](https://m.investing.com/news/economy-news/us-manufacturing-sector-grows-in-march-supplier-delivery-performance-deteriorates-4593467?ampMode=1), [AP](https://apnews.com/article/6fc90a2e50b1252cde130fc3e0ce0da3).

That leaves BTC in a better tactical spot than ETH, but not in a clean breakout setup. Yahoo Finance said Bitcoin traded around $66,694 and Ethereum around $1,918 on April 1, both lower on the day. That matters less as an isolated price move than as a role test: crypto did not act like the obvious safe-haven winner when geopolitical risk came back into focus. Source: [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/crypto-price-today-bitcoin-ethereum-xrp-solana-fall-trump-unleashes-tariff-meltdown-across-markets-154252864.html).

The constructive part is that institutional flow support has not obviously broken. Farside's latest visible U.S. ETF tables still show positive net daily flow context for both spot Bitcoin ETFs and spot Ethereum ETFs, with Bitcoin's demand base clearly larger. That means spot weakness is happening against a still-stable real-money flow backdrop. Sources: [Farside BTC](https://farside.co.uk/btc/), [Farside ETH](https://farside.co.uk/eth/).

My judgment for the next 24 hours to 3 sessions is that BTC still has the cleaner setup, but only if payrolls on April 3 help drag yields and the dollar lower. ETH can participate if macro improves, but it still lacks a stronger independent demand impulse. If the market keeps trading higher oil and sticky inflation instead of weaker growth, both BTC and ETH can stay heavy, with ETH more vulnerable.
