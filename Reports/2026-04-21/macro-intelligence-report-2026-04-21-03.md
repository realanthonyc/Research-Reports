## US Macro Environment

Growth still looks resilient enough to avoid a clean recession call. February retail sales were up 0.6% month over month, and the market is waiting for March data to confirm whether higher gasoline prices have started crowding out discretionary spending. Inflation is less comfortable: headline CPI accelerated because of energy, while core CPI remained calmer. The Fed interpretation is therefore conditional. If energy stays elevated but core is contained, the Fed can wait. If higher energy leaks into expectations, the market should price fewer cuts and a higher term premium.

For equities, this creates a late-cycle but not yet broken setup. Strong data is not automatically bullish when oil is high because strong consumption plus energy inflation can lift yields. Weak data is not automatically bullish either because it can trigger stagflation fears. The market needs either lower oil or clear evidence that consumers and margins can absorb the shock.
