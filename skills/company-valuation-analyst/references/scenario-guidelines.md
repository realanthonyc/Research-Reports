# Scenario Guidelines

Use these guidelines to choose the valuation frame and decide what each scenario should flex.

## Match Method To Company Type

| Company type | Primary method | Secondary check |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Stable cash-generative business | DCF, FCF yield, P/E | EV/EBITDA |
| High-growth reinvestment story | EV/Sales, long-term margin bridge | DCF with cautious terminal assumptions |
| Cyclical business | Mid-cycle earnings or EBITDA | Asset value, replacement cost, book value |
| Asset-heavy company | NAV, asset value, liquidation or private-market comparables | Earnings power |
| Turnaround | Probability-weighted earnings power | Sum of downside floor and recovery value |
| Event-driven / binary outcome | Probability-adjusted scenario value | Downside floor |
| Multi-segment business | SOTP | Consolidated multiple |

## What To Flex In Bear / Base / Bull

- Revenue growth or unit volume
- Gross margin and operating margin
- Capital intensity and cash conversion
- Multiple or discount rate
- Cycle timing
- Asset monetization timing
- Dilution / buyback / debt paydown assumptions
- Success probability when the business is event-driven

## Minimum Discipline

- Do not change ten variables at once without saying which 2-3 variables matter most.
- Keep bear/base/bull intuitively distinct.
- Use the same units and valuation basis across scenarios.
- Explain why the chosen method fits the company better than the obvious alternatives.

## When To Use Probability-Weighted Logic

Use probability-weighted valuation when a large part of equity value depends on one or two events:

- regulatory decision
- major product launch
- litigation
- refinancing
- asset sale
- restructuring
- one large contract or customer outcome

In those cases, show the downside floor separately from upside case value.
