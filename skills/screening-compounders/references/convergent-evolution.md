# Convergent Evolution Test

Use an external lens. The question is not "why is this company unique?" but "has this business model already produced repeat winners elsewhere?"

## What counts as a pass

The business model should resemble a proven pattern with these traits:
- winner-take-most or winner-take-all tendency
- high and durable margins or returns on capital
- strong recurring or repeat cash generation
- advantages that deepen with scale, density, habit, switching costs, or brand trust

## Good example patterns

- membership-led scale retail
- dominant exchanges / market infrastructure
- mission-critical enterprise software with high switching costs
- payment rails / networks
- branded consumer staples with distribution strength
- industrial or data businesses with embedded workflow lock-in

## Weak examples

- "This founder is brilliant"
- "This product is viral right now"
- "This company is unlike anything else"

Those are internal-view claims, not convergent-evolution evidence.

## How to document it

For each surviving company, list:
- the underlying business model
- at least 2 comparable winners from other markets, periods, or geographies when possible
- why those cases prove the model rather than just the company
- whether the playbook historically ends in high profits and cash flow

## Failure condition

Fail if the thesis depends mainly on a one-off story that has not repeatedly worked elsewhere.
