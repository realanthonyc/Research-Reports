## Detailed Summary

BTC and ETH are holding up, but the market is not trading them as pure safe havens. The strongest BTC-specific confirmed development is Strategy's April 20 disclosure that it acquired 34,164 BTC and now holds 815,061 BTC. That keeps the corporate treasury bid alive and gives BTC a demand narrative while spot trades around the mid-$75,000 to $76,000 area in current market coverage. Sources: [Strategy](https://www.strategy.com/press/strategy-acquires-34164-btc-and-now-holds-815061-btc_04-20-2026), [FX Leaders](https://www.fxleaders.com/news/2026/04/21/daily-crypto-signals-bitcoin-holds-above-76k-ethereums-institutional-demand-rises/).

ETH has a more complicated setup. ETH treasury demand is being highlighted by current crypto coverage, but the Kelp DAO / rsETH exploit has created a DeFi risk premium that is directly relevant to ETH because stolen or unbacked rsETH collateral reportedly affected Aave, Compound and other lending venues. The trader read is: ETH can outperform if treasury and ETF-flow demand dominates, but it should carry a higher ecosystem-risk discount until lending-protocol contagion is contained. Sources: [FX Leaders](https://www.fxleaders.com/news/2026/04/21/daily-crypto-signals-bitcoin-holds-above-76k-ethereums-institutional-demand-rises/), [MEXC](https://www.mexc.com/crypto-pulse/article/293m-gone-in-46-minutes-105776).

Macro is the swing factor for both assets. Hormuz risk pushed oil higher on April 20, while the 10-year Treasury yield was around 4.25% and DXY near 98.07. If oil keeps yields and the dollar firm, BTC/ETH should trade more like liquidity beta than inflation hedges. If oil stabilizes and DXY softens after retail sales or Warsh testimony, BTC can benefit first through the treasury-bid narrative, while ETH needs DeFi stress to stop widening. Sources: [MarketWatch oil](https://www.marketwatch.com/livecoverage/s-p-500-dow-jones-nasdaq-oil-prices-war-in-iran-strait-of-hormuz-opened-closed/card/s-p-500-set-to-fall-as-oil-advances-WxzBSgeyfIHRV0RPknfG), [MarketWatch yields/DXY](https://www.marketwatch.com/livecoverage/s-p-500-dow-jones-nasdaq-oil-prices-war-in-iran-strait-of-hormuz-opened-closed/card/treasury-yields-tick-higher-as-u-s-dollar-climbs-4gNISihWRBUqivGkF7tj).
