## Detailed Summary

No high-confidence BTC-native or ETH-native headline displaced macro as the main driver in the last 24 hours. The tradable setup is still being controlled by the March U.S. payrolls release from Friday, April 3, the post-payroll rates-and-dollar reaction, and the persistence of oil/geopolitical stress into the weekend. The Bureau of Labor Statistics said payrolls rose by 178,000 in March, unemployment was 4.3%, and average hourly earnings rose 0.2% month over month and 3.5% year over year. That weakens the immediate case for an aggressive Fed pivot without creating a new wage-driven inflation panic. Source: [BLS Employment Situation](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.htm).

The macro transmission path for crypto remains straightforward. Firmer payrolls -> less urgency for cuts -> firmer yields and dollar -> higher hurdle for BTC and ETH into the new week. The Federal Reserve's latest H.15 release showed the 2-year Treasury constant maturity yield at 3.79% and the 10-year at 4.31% as of April 2, while Investing's Sunday evening reference snapshot showed Dollar Index futures at 100.080, up 0.22%. That is not a hostile enough setup to force liquidation by itself, but it is restrictive enough to cap upside unless the dollar stalls. Sources: [Federal Reserve H.15](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h15/), [Investing Dollar Index](https://www.investing.com/currencies/us-dollar-index).

The cleanest crypto-specific signal is still the ETF-flow split. Farside showed U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded a $9.0 million net inflow on April 2, while U.S. spot Ethereum ETFs recorded a $71.2 million net outflow. That matters more than a single weekend candle because it tells us where institutional demand is actually choosing to sit when macro conditions are not easy. Sources: [Farside BTC](https://farside.co.uk/btc/), [Farside ETH](https://farside.co.uk/eth/).

My judgment for the next 24 hours to 3 sessions is that BTC still has the cleaner setup than ETH, but not a breakout-quality setup. BTC can defend and potentially outperform if DXY fails to extend and Monday risk markets reopen without a fresh geopolitical shock. ETH still looks like the weaker macro beta because the ETF complex is not yet providing a sponsorship floor.
