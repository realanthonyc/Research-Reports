## Detailed Summary

The most important thing for BTC and ETH in the latest window is that the March 18 FOMC did not give crypto the liquidity relief it wanted. The Fed held rates at `3.5%-3.75%`, but the new projections showed 2026 inflation medians moving higher rather than lower. That matters because crypto, especially ETH, still trades through the rates / dollar channel. When the market hears “sticky inflation, gradual policy easing at best,” BTC and ETH can still rally tactically, but they struggle to sustain a broad beta expansion. [Federal Reserve press-release index](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/2026-press-fomc.htm) [Federal Reserve SEP](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcprojtabl20260318.htm)

The immediate market reaction confirmed that message. Barron’s reported BTC down `3.9%` to `US$71,678` after the Fed, while ETH fell `5.2%`. That relative performance matters. BTC fell, but ETH fell more. In the current macro regime, BTC still behaves like the cleaner institutional and macro-reserve crypto expression, while ETH trades more like higher-beta crypto equity. If the market does not get lower yields or a softer dollar, ETH usually has more trouble keeping up. [Barron's](https://www.barrons.com/articles/bitcoin-xrp-ethereum-fed-decision-cryptos-powell-e3021a22)

This does not mean the crypto backdrop is uniformly bearish. CoinShares’ latest weekly fund-flow report still showed `US$646m` of net inflows into digital-asset products, including `US$521m` into BTC and `US$88.5m` into ETH. That means institutional demand has not disappeared; it just is not strong enough on its own to overpower a restrictive macro backdrop. The correct read is therefore `selective, not euphoric`. BTC still has a credible case for relative resilience if yields and DXY stabilize. ETH still needs either a more obvious risk-on shift or an ETH-specific catalyst, and there was no sufficiently strong fresh ETH-native catalyst in the latest window. [CoinShares](https://coinshares.com/insights/research-data/fund-flows-09-03-26/)

My judgment for the next one to three sessions is that BTC remains tactically preferable to ETH unless the post-Fed macro read improves materially. If the `2Y` yield and `DXY` stop pushing tighter, BTC can recover first. If rates and the dollar remain firm, ETH likely continues to lag because it carries more pure beta sensitivity.
