My judgment is that capital is moving out of the most crowded `energy panic + defensive dollar + narrow hiding places` trade and back into broader equities, crypto beta, and rate-sensitive exposures. But this is still a fragile relief regime, not a durable all-clear. If the diplomatic path wobbles and oil re-prices higher again, the same market will quickly go back to trading sticky inflation, tighter policy, and weaker real-income expectations.

## US Macro Environment

The U.S. backdrop is a `late-cycle economy dealing with an energy shock that may be easing before it fully hardens into policy`. The inflation prints were not friendly, but the market chose to look through them once oil broke lower. That is rational only if traders believe the crude spike was temporary and the April/May inflation path will look better than the backward-looking March data. Sources: [BLS CPI, April 10](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_04102026.htm), [BLS PPI, April 14](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ppi.nr0.htm).

The Fed therefore remains on a `hawkish hold with optionality`, not on an active easing path. Market pricing can drift toward late-2026 cuts without the Fed endorsing them immediately. The practical implication is that duration-sensitive and high-beta assets can still rally near term, but only so long as the energy shock keeps receding.
