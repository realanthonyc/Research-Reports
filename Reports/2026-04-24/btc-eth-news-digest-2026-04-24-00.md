## Detailed Summary

The main near-term story is still institutional demand, not a broad retail-led crypto beta chase. BTC held near the high-$77,000 to $78,000 area after a sharp geopolitical-risk rebound, while U.S. spot bitcoin ETF demand remained the clearest support mechanism. The Block reported, using SoSoValue, that spot bitcoin ETFs had drawn over $2 billion across an eight-day positive streak and $223.2 million on Thursday, April 23, led by IBIT. This creates a constructive but fragile setup: ETF demand can keep dips shallow, but a large part of the near-term price debate is now whether BTC can sustain acceptance above roughly $78,000 and force an $80,000 test rather than simply consolidate after a fast move.

ETH is showing a different pattern. Reported ETH ETF inflow streaks suggest institutional appetite is improving, but ETH price action remains capped near the $2,300-$2,400 zone. That divergence matters because it tells traders not to assume ETH will automatically outperform if BTC ETF flows remain strong. ETH has a more mixed tape: ETF demand is a positive flow signal, but weaker DApp revenue commentary and repeated rejection below $2,400 keep the near-term bias more neutral than BTC unless ETH reclaims that level with volume.

Macro is not quiet. The next scheduled FOMC decision is April 29, 2026, and the official Fed calendar confirms the April 28-29 meeting. The March minutes already described elevated uncertainty and Middle East-related downside risks. For BTC and ETH, the immediate transmission path is rates, the U.S. dollar, oil/inflation expectations, and risk appetite. My base-case inference for the next one to three sessions: BTC has a better upside asymmetry than ETH while ETF inflows persist, but both assets are vulnerable to a hawkish pre-FOMC rates/USD repricing or renewed Middle East oil shock.
