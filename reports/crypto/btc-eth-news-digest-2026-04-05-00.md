## Detailed Summary

There was no major new BTC- or ETH-native catalyst between the prior U.S. close and the current Tokyo Sunday / New York Saturday weekend check. The tradable setup is still being dominated by macro. The key confirmed macro event remains the March U.S. payrolls report released on April 3. The Bureau of Labor Statistics said payrolls rose by 178,000, unemployment was 4.3%, and average hourly earnings rose 0.2% month over month and 3.5% year over year. That matters because it weakens the case for an immediate Fed pivot without creating a clean wage-driven inflation scare. Source: [BLS Employment Situation](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.htm).

For BTC and ETH, the transmission chain is still straightforward. Firmer payrolls push yields and the dollar modestly higher, which raises the macro hurdle for crypto into the weekend. The Federal Reserve's latest H.15 release showed the 2-year Treasury constant maturity yield at 3.79% and the 10-year at 4.31% as of April 2, while the broader market tone stayed tighter rather than easier after the jobs release. Oil also remained elevated enough to keep inflation and risk-sentiment uncertainty alive. Sources: [Federal Reserve H.15](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h15/), [AP market recap](https://apnews.com/article/financial-markets-oil-prices-iran-trump-cbf38b67032e2fae95073f4fbcc0ca24).

The cleaner crypto-specific signal is still ETF flow divergence. Farside showed U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded a $9.0 million net inflow on April 2 after a $173.7 million net outflow on April 1. U.S. spot Ethereum ETFs showed a $71.2 million net outflow on April 2 after a $7.1 million net outflow on April 1. That matters because it says institutional demand is selective rather than broad. BTC is still attracting opportunistic dip-buying. ETH is still being treated as weaker macro beta. Sources: [Farside BTC](https://farside.co.uk/btc/), [Farside ETH](https://farside.co.uk/eth/).

My judgment for the next 24 hours to 3 sessions is that BTC still has the cleaner setup than ETH, but conviction should stay moderate because the weekend can only help BTC if the dollar stops strengthening and if there is no fresh geopolitical escalation. If DXY extends and oil stays high, ETH should remain the weaker leg and BTC should at best defend rather than trend.
