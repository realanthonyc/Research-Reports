The short version is that crypto had a relatively constructive 24 hours, but most of the signal came from macro rather than fresh BTC- or ETH-native headlines. The macro backdrop is doing most of the work. Oil remained above $100, but Treasury yields fell sharply into the March 30 close. The U.S. Treasury curve showed the 2-year at 3.82% and the 10-year at 4.35%, down from 3.88% and 4.44% on March 27, while the 10-year real yield fell to 2.04% from 2.13%. That combination usually helps non-sovereign stores of value because it lowers real-rate pressure even if headline geopolitical risk stays high. BTC is benefiting more cleanly from that than ETH because ETH still faces a softer relative-flow backdrop after March 23 weekly fund data showed Bitcoin inflows but Ethereum outflows. My trading judgment for the next 24 hours to 3 sessions is that BTC has the cleaner tactical setup than ETH. BTC is showing relative-strength behavior versus equities, and recent institutional flow evidence remains broadly supportive even after the post-FOMC slowdown. ETH is not broken, but its flow story is more mixed: structurally it has support from the new U.S. staking ETF complex, including BlackRock’s ETHB launch in March, but near-term it still behaves like a higher-beta expression that needs risk appetite to remain stable.
