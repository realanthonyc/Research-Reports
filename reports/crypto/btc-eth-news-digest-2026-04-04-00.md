## Detailed Summary

The latest BTC/ETH setup is still being dictated by macro more than by crypto-native headlines. The key new macro event was the U.S. March payrolls report on April 3. The Bureau of Labor Statistics said payrolls rose by 178,000, the unemployment rate was 4.3%, and average hourly earnings rose 0.2% month over month and 3.5% year over year. That mix is important because it weakens the immediate case for a fast Fed pivot without creating a clean inflation panic through wages alone. Source: [BLS Employment Situation](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.htm).

For BTC and ETH, the transmission chain is straightforward. Firmer payrolls pushed Treasury yields and the dollar modestly higher, which raised the macro hurdle for crypto into the weekend. The U.S. 10-year yield closed at 4.345%, the 2-year at 3.846%, and DXY at 99.990. Gold fell 0.48% to 4,679.70. Those are not crisis numbers, but they are consistent with a less-friendly liquidity backdrop for high-beta assets. Sources: [Investing 10Y](https://www.investing.com/rates-bonds/u.s.-10-year-bond-yield), [Investing 2Y](https://www.investing.com/rates-bonds/u.s.-2-year-bond-yield), [Investing DXY](https://www.investing.com/currencies/us-dollar-index), [Investing gold](https://www.investing.com/commodities/gold).

The more interesting signal is in crypto flows. Bitcoin's spot ETF tape improved from a large outflow to a small inflow, while Ethereum's spot ETF tape deteriorated further. Farside showed U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded a $9.0 million net inflow on April 2 after a $173.7 million net outflow on April 1. U.S. spot Ethereum ETFs showed a $71.2 million net outflow on April 2 after a $7.1 million net outflow on April 1. That split matters because it says institutional demand is selective rather than broad. Sources: [Farside BTC](https://farside.co.uk/btc/), [Farside ETH](https://farside.co.uk/eth/).

My judgment for the next 24 hours to 3 sessions is that BTC still has the cleaner setup than ETH. Bitcoin is not obviously strong, but it has at least some evidence of dip-buying. Ethereum still looks more like weak macro beta with thinner institutional sponsorship. If DXY fails to break higher and Monday risk sentiment stabilizes, BTC can recover first. If the dollar extends, oil remains elevated, or weekend headlines worsen, ETH should remain the weaker leg.
