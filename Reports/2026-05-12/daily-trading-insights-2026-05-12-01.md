## Detailed Summary

The market starts Tuesday with strong index momentum but a dense macro event wall. Monday’s U.S. session closed with fresh records: S&P 500 7,412.84, Dow 49,704.47, Nasdaq 26,274.13, and Russell 2000 2,870.64 ([AP](https://apnews.com/article/ddf210e6c5243dcf5430a4667457f371)). The rally was not broad: only 37.8% of U.S. issues advanced, while semiconductors, computer hardware, and energy carried the tape ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-may-11-2026-updates)).

Today’s trading problem is whether CPI and the 10Y auction validate or break the AI-led rally. April CPI is due at 8:30 a.m. ET on 2026-05-12 ([BLS](https://www.bls.gov/schedule/news_release/cpi.htm?lv=true)); the $42B 10Y auction follows at 1:00 p.m. ET ([Treasury](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0489)). If CPI is manageable and the auction clears well, QQQ, SMH, BTC-linked equities, and small caps can squeeze higher. If CPI is hot and the auction tails, the best setups shift toward energy, dollar/yield hedges, and reduced exposure to crowded duration.
