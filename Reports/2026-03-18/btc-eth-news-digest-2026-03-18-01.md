## Detailed Summary

The most important thing in crypto over the last 24 hours is not a clean BTC- or ETH-native catalyst. It is the interaction between improving institutional flow tone and a still-fragile macro backdrop. On the supportive side, CoinShares reported another strong week of inflows into digital-asset products, with `US$646m` total inflows, including `US$521m` into Bitcoin and `US$88.5m` into Ethereum products. That confirms institutional demand did not break during the recent volatility. On the restrictive side, oil has moved back above `US$100`, the market is waiting for the March `17-18` FOMC outcome, and that combination keeps the rates / USD channel from fully turning risk-on. [CoinShares](https://coinshares.com/insights/research-data/fund-flows-09-03-26/) [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomccalendars.htm) [MarketWatch](https://www.marketwatch.com/livecoverage/stock-market-today-dow-sp500-nasdaq-lower-as-brent-oil-above-100/card/s-p-500-futures-dip-as-brent-crude-goes-back-above-100-a-barrel-dtnlakg8Ell1KpRK6cGc)

BTC is therefore acting like the cleaner macro expression. Barron’s reported BTC around `US$73,984-74,038` while ETH traded closer to `US$1,772-1,794`, with both stabilizing as traders weighed Iran-related risk, oil, and the Fed. The fact that BTC has held up better than ETH fits the current regime: when macro uncertainty rises, the market tends to prefer the most liquid, institutionally accepted crypto asset first. ETH is still getting support from positive flow data, but the absence of a fresh ETH-specific catalyst means it is more dependent on broad risk conditions and less able to lead on its own. [Barron's 1](https://www.barrons.com/articles/bitcoin-ethereum-xrp-crypto-iran-risk-62e28edb) [Barron's 2](https://www.barrons.com/articles/bitcoin-price-fed-cryptos-xrp-ethereum-eabec824)

My judgment for the next one to three sessions is: BTC remains modestly favored over ETH unless the Fed outcome and oil path become decisively risk-friendly. If oil stabilizes and the Fed does not harden the market’s inflation fears, both can grind higher, but BTC still has the cleaner setup. If the macro tape tightens again through a stronger dollar and higher yields, ETH is more exposed to lag.
