## Detailed Summary

This run remains `coverage-thin for fresh BTC/ETH-native headlines, but high-signal on macro transmission`. The dominant change in the last 24 hours was the March 23 cross-asset relief move after President Donald Trump said the United States would delay planned strikes on Iranian power infrastructure for five days. AP reported Brent collapsed `10.9%` to `US$99.94/bbl`, while U.S. stocks rallied. For BTC and ETH, the immediate importance is not politics by itself; it is the mechanical path: `less oil panic -> less near-term inflation fear -> less upward pressure on yields / dollar -> better conditions for crypto beta`. [AP market wrap, March 23](https://apnews.com/article/026e3ab83a6256e36001b85058f92b5d) [AP Trump/Iran skepticism, March 23](https://apnews.com/article/e27ba37004edf6dc241417ad11c87760)

The problem is that the underlying macro regime is still not easy. The Fed held rates at `3.50%-3.75%` on March 18, the March SEP kept 2026 `PCE` and `core PCE` medians at `2.7%`, and the March 20 `H.15` still showed a `3.79%` `2Y`, `4.25%` `10Y`, and `1.88%` `10Y` real yield. So crypto is getting relief from a lower oil shock, not from a newly dovish policy backdrop. That distinction matters because it argues for stabilization first, breakout later. [Federal Reserve statement, March 18](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260318a.htm) [Federal Reserve SEP, March 18](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcprojtabl20260318.htm) [Federal Reserve H.15](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h15/)

BTC still looks cleaner than ETH on this tape. MarketWatch reported Bitcoin dropped below `US$68,000` in the early March 23 risk-off phase, but Farside's March 23 flow table then showed spot-Bitcoin ETF outflows of only `-US$35.3 million`, better than the `-US$52.0 million` on March 20. ETH did not get the same recovery signal from ETF demand: Farside showed spot-Ethereum ETF flows at `US$0.0 million` on March 23 after `-US$42.0 million` on March 20. That is not bearish enough to be panic, but it is also not the kind of institutional re-risking that normally fuels ETH outperformance. [MarketWatch futures coverage, March 23](https://www.marketwatch.com/story/u-s-stock-futures-sink-as-trump-and-iran-trade-threats-against-civilian-infrastructure-baae06ba) [Farside Bitcoin ETF flows](https://farside.co.uk/btc/) [Farside Ethereum ETF flows](https://farside.co.uk/eth/)

My base case for the next `24 hours to 3 sessions` is `BTC steadier than ETH, with both still hostage to oil/yield headlines`. If oil stays below `US$100` and the next U.S. data do not revive inflation concerns, BTC can keep repairing and ETH can follow. If the Iran headline turns out to be noise and oil snaps back, crypto will likely revert to macro-beta behavior very quickly.
