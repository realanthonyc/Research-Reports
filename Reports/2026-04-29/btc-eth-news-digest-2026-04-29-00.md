## Detailed Summary

BTC and ETH are trading as macro-sensitive risk assets into the April 28-29 FOMC decision, with the crypto-specific tape showing a clear cooling in spot ETF demand after a strong mid-April inflow run. BTC is near $77,000 and ETH is near $2,300 as of the April 29 Asia/Europe session, while U.S. 10-year yields are around 4.36% before the Fed decision at 2:00 p.m. ET. The practical implication is simple: the next directional move is less about a surprise rate decision and more about whether Fed communication validates "higher for longer" rates or gives risk assets room to breathe. The base case is range-bound, two-way volatility before the decision, then a higher probability of trend extension after Powell's press conference if yields, DXY, and ETF flows align.

The most tradable crypto-native change is the ETF flow reversal. U.S. spot BTC ETFs saw a $263 million outflow on April 27 after nine sessions of inflows, then a smaller $89.7 million outflow on April 28, according to secondary reports citing SoSoValue/Farside data. That does not erase the prior $2.1 billion inflow streak, but it changes the short-term signal from "persistent bid" to "institutional demand needs confirmation." For ETH, corporate treasury accumulation remains a supportive structural narrative, with Bitmine reporting 5.078 million ETH held as of April 26, but that is slower-moving support and not enough by itself to offset macro risk if yields and the dollar tighten financial conditions.
