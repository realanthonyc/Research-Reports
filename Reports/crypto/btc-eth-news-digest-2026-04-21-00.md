## Detailed Summary

BTC and ETH are trading as liquidity-sensitive macro assets rather than isolated crypto stories. The latest Yahoo market snapshot showed BTC near 74,884.95, down 1.19%, and ETH near 2,297.26, down 2.39%, while U.S. equities remained strong and VIX sat at 17.48 ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/calendar/earnings?from=2026-04-21&to=2026-04-27)). BTC recently failed to hold the full extension toward the 78,000 area and consolidated near 74k-75k as Hormuz/geopolitical risk weighed on sentiment ([Economic Times](https://m.economictimes.com/markets/cryptocurrency/bitcoin-consolidates-near-74500-after-hitting-10-week-high-of-78000-as-hormuz-tensions-weigh-on-sentiment/articleshow/130387975.cms)).

The key judgment: BTC has the stronger institutional-flow backstop, while ETH has better upside beta if risk appetite stabilizes. Farside shows BTC ETF flows were very strong on 2026-04-17 at +$663.9m, then modestly negative on 2026-04-20 at -$17.6m; ETH ETF flows were +$127.4m on 2026-04-17 and -$21.5m on 2026-04-20 ([Farside BTC](https://farside.co.uk/btc/), [Farside ETH](https://farside.co.uk/eth/)). That tells us the ETF bid is not broken, but it is not a straight-line daily inflow either.

Macro is the short-term driver. Renewed Strait of Hormuz disruption is pressuring oil and inflation expectations, lifting the risk that yields and DXY rise together. That is usually bad for BTC/ETH because it tightens liquidity and reduces appetite for high-beta assets. The base case for the next 1-3 sessions is range trading unless oil stabilizes or ETF inflows re-accelerate. A clean BTC break above the recent 78k high area likely needs both risk-on equities and positive BTC ETF flows; ETH likely needs BTC stability plus stronger ETHA/FETH-led inflows.
