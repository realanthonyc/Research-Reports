# Source Priority

Use this reference to rank evidence quality for Daily Trading Insights.

## Primary Sources

Prefer these for factual claims:
- Company IR releases, SEC filings, earnings transcripts, investor presentations
- Official economic releases: BLS, BEA, Census, ISM/S&P Global PMI, Treasury, Federal Reserve, regional Fed banks
- Regulator or government releases: SEC, CFTC, FTC, DoJ, White House, Congress, state regulators, foreign regulators when relevant
- Exchange, ETF issuer, index provider, or official fund-flow publications
- Official project, protocol, product, or executive-company communications when market-moving

Confidence impact: usually High if current, directly relevant, and not contradicted.

## High-Quality Secondary Sources

Use these for context, market reaction, and interpretation:
- Reuters, Bloomberg, WSJ, Financial Times, CNBC, MarketWatch, Barron's, AP, official exchange newsrooms
- Reputable specialist publications for specific sectors
- Sell-side notes only when the firm, analyst action, and market relevance are clearly identified

Confidence impact: usually Medium to High depending on corroboration.

## Market And Flow Sources

Use carefully:
- ETF flow trackers, options analytics, exchange open interest, funding, short interest, dark-pool or block-trade commentary
- Fund-manager surveys, positioning notes, institutional commentary
- 13F, Form 4, Schedule 13D/G, and other filings

Confidence impact: Medium for verified data, Low to Medium for interpretation. Never call flow "smart money proof."

## Social And Rumor Sources

Use only when market-moving or confirmed elsewhere:
- Executive posts
- Company/product social accounts
- Credible journalists' posts
- Widely circulating rumors

Confidence impact: Low unless confirmed by primary or high-quality secondary sources. Label clearly as developing or unconfirmed.

## Confidence Labels

- `High`: primary source or multiple high-quality confirmations; clear date/timestamp; direct market relevance.
- `Medium`: credible source and plausible transmission path, but interpretation or market reaction still developing.
- `Low`: rumor, single-source social claim, weak flow clue, or plausible but unconfirmed implication.
