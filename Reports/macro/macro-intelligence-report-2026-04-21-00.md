Waller's 2026-04-17 speech adds nuance: slower labor-force growth means low payroll numbers may not mean recession as quickly as in prior cycles, but weaker hiring and job-finding rates still leave the labor market vulnerable.

The Fed therefore has asymmetric communication risk: it can acknowledge soft labor and tariff-adjusted underlying inflation, but if it sounds too dovish while oil is rising, long-end yields may sell off rather than rally.
