# Global Macro Intelligence - 2026-05-04

## Detailed Summary

Analysis date: 2026-05-04. User timezone: Asia/Tokyo. U.S. market date: Monday, 2026-05-04.

The global macro regime is best described as **risk-on but inflation-fragile**. U.S. equities are still being supported by strong earnings, broad AI infrastructure demand, and core-index ETF inflows, but the inflation shock from the U.S.-Iran / Strait of Hormuz disruption is preventing the bond market from treating slower growth risk as cleanly bullish. The key transmission chain is: **oil supply stress -> higher inflation risk premium -> higher 2Y/10Y yields and more hawkish central-bank pricing -> stronger or less-easy dollar liquidity -> valuation pressure on long-duration growth**, partially offset by **hyperscaler capex and cloud revenue -> AI supply-chain earnings support -> mega-cap and semiconductor leadership**.

The most important recent macro facts are: the Fed held the funds target range at **3.50%-3.75%** on 2026-04-29 and explicitly cited elevated inflation partly from global energy prices ([Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm)); BEA reported Q1 real GDP growth of **2.0% annualized** and March PCE inflation of **3.5% y/y**, with core PCE at **3.2% y/y** ([BEA GDP](https://www.bea.gov/news/2026/gdp-advance-estimate-1st-quarter-2026), [BEA PCE](https://www.bea.gov/news/2026/personal-income-and-outlays-march-2026)); Treasury yields were rising early 2026-05-04, with the 10Y near **4.400%** and 2Y near **3.909%** as markets focused on inflation and Friday payrolls ([WSJ](https://www.wsj.com/articles/treasury-yields-steady-as-oil-trades-well-below-thursdays-peak-99a78de1)).

My forward judgment for the next **1-2 weeks**: the base case is not a classic risk-off unwind. It is a **selective risk-on tape with a tighter discount-rate ceiling**. Equities can keep grinding if AI earnings and labor data hold up, but duration-heavy assets become vulnerable whenever oil, DXY, or yields rise together. Gold and Bitcoin remain macro hedges with different drivers: gold is being supported by geopolitical and reserve-diversification demand despite rate pressure, while Bitcoin needs ETF inflows and a break above the $80K supply zone to confirm that liquidity appetite is broadening.

## Brief Summary

- Regime: risk-on, inflationary, growth-resilient, liquidity not clearly expanding.
- Main driver: AI/cloud earnings support is fighting oil-driven rate repricing.
- Just happened: Fed held rates; BEA showed resilient Q1 growth and sticky March inflation; Japan intervened or was suspected of intervening to support the yen.
- Waiting for: ISM Services and JOLTS on 2026-05-05, jobless claims on 2026-05-07, nonfarm payrolls on 2026-05-08.
- Capital rotation: inflows favor core U.S. equity beta, semiconductors, financials, and some Bitcoin exposure; defensives and gold still have hedge demand.
- Smart-money read: stronger hands appear to be adding selective U.S. large-cap/AI beta while maintaining inflation/geopolitical hedges rather than abandoning risk.

## Major Recent Developments

### Just Happened: Fed Holds But Drops Comfort With Inflation

Fact: On 2026-04-29, the FOMC kept the target range at **3.50%-3.75%** and said inflation is elevated, partly reflecting the global energy-price increase ([Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm)). The implementation note set interest on reserve balances at **3.65%** and overnight repo at **3.75%** ([Federal Reserve implementation note](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a1.htm)).

Inference: The Fed is no longer able to lean dovish on slower growth unless energy pressure fades. The front end should remain data-sensitive, with labor data this week more important than usual.

Judgment: Equities can tolerate a steady Fed, but not a Fed forced to reprice toward hikes by oil. The inflation channel is now the main risk to the risk-on tape.

### Recent: U.S. Growth Improved, But Inflation Stayed Sticky

Fact: BEA reported Q1 real GDP growth at **2.0% annualized**, up from **0.5%** in Q4 2025, with investment, exports, consumption, and government spending all contributing ([BEA GDP](https://www.bea.gov/news/2026/gdp-advance-estimate-1st-quarter-2026)). March personal income rose **0.6%**, nominal PCE rose **0.9%**, real PCE rose **0.2%**, and the PCE price index rose **0.7% m/m** and **3.5% y/y** ([BEA PCE](https://www.bea.gov/news/2026/personal-income-and-outlays-march-2026)).

Inference: Demand is not collapsing, but real disposable income fell **0.1%** in March. That mix can keep earnings resilient while keeping the Fed cautious.

### Upcoming: Labor Week

The 2026-05-04 to 2026-05-08 calendar includes factory orders, ISM Services, JOLTS, jobless claims, and April nonfarm payrolls. FHLB New York lists consensus for NFP at **63K** versus **178K** prior, ISM Services at **53.7** versus **54.0**, and JOLTS at **6.700M** versus **6.882M** ([FHLB New York](https://www.fhlbny.com/w/fi/msd/2026/260501)). Kiplinger also flags JOLTS, claims, ADP, ISM Services, and NFP as the week’s key macro tests ([Kiplinger](https://www.kiplinger.com/investing/economy/this-weeks-economic-calendar)).

## US Macro Environment

Growth is resilient but uneven. Q1 GDP acceleration and strong nominal spending keep the soft-landing narrative alive, but high oil and March PCE inflation make it hard for the Fed to validate aggressive rate-cut expectations. The labor market is the next regime checkpoint: a mild payroll slowdown would help risk assets by easing yields; a sharp downside payroll miss would flip the market from “rate relief” into “earnings-risk” mode; a hot wage/payroll print would pressure the front end and high-multiple growth.

For the next **1-3 U.S. trading sessions**, the market is likely to treat good-but-not-hot data as bullish. Hot inflation or wage evidence is bearish because it would reinforce the higher-for-longer channel. Very weak labor data is ambiguous: bullish for Treasuries and gold, but bearish for cyclicals and small caps if credit spreads widen.

Geopolitics: The Strait of Hormuz remains the central macro shock. Markets are trading every update through oil, inflation expectations, central-bank pricing, and margin pressure.

## Interest Rates And Liquidity

The 2Y near **3.909%** and 10Y near **4.400%** on 2026-05-04 signal a market that is not fully buying imminent disinflation relief ([WSJ](https://www.wsj.com/articles/treasury-yields-steady-as-oil-trades-well-below-thursdays-peak-99a78de1)). The curve remains positively sloped but the pressure is not purely growth optimism; it includes inflation and term-premium risk.

Policy liquidity is neutral-to-tight: the Fed is on hold, not easing. System liquidity is not showing a fresh rescue impulse. Market liquidity is still adequate because equities and ETF flows are absorbing supply, but it is conditional on contained oil and credit spreads.

## Dollar And FX Dynamics

DXY behavior should be read as a regime check rather than a side datapoint. A firm dollar alongside rising yields would pressure commodities in normal conditions, but oil/geopolitical stress is keeping gold and energy supported. Yen intervention complicates the dollar story: if Japan repeatedly defends USD/JPY near 160, it can interrupt carry trades and tighten global financial conditions even without a broad DXY breakout.

## Commodities

Oil is the macro switch. WTI reached **$110.10** intraweek and closed Friday at **$102.48**, while Brent reached **$120.65** and closed at **$108.72**, according to a 2026-05-04 Yardeni/Investing.com week-ahead note ([Investing.com Canada](https://ca.investing.com/analysis/economic-week-ahead-adp-payrolls-layoffs-jobless-claims-to-shape-outlook-200624200)). If oil stays above $100, the market’s rate-cut narrative is capped.

Gold is not just a safe haven. On 2026-05-04, gold fell in thin trade near **$4,597.70** before later edging around **$4,617.35**, reflecting the tug-of-war between higher-rate risk and geopolitical/financial-stability demand ([WSJ](https://www.wsj.com/finance/commodities-futures/gold-edges-higher-amid-signs-of-resilience-627248e3)). The stronger structural story remains central-bank demand, reserve diversification, and geopolitical hedging.

China’s stronger PMI is constructive for copper and industrial metals, but the same energy-cost shock threatens margins. Silver remains a hybrid: it can benefit from precious-metal demand but is vulnerable if growth expectations fall.

## Cross-Asset Macro Interpretation

Equities, gold, and BTC are all bid for different reasons, while bonds are resisting the bullish narrative. That is a mixed but still constructive regime. The strongest contradiction is: equities say earnings/liquidity are good enough; Treasuries say inflation and policy risk are not solved. The market can live with this contradiction while credit is calm and oil is stable. It becomes unstable if oil rises, DXY rises, and breadth narrows at the same time.

## Capital Rotation And Smart-Money Read

ETF.com reported broad positive daily ETF flows on 2026-04-29, led by large-cap U.S. equity exposure, with IVV receiving more than **$3.1B**, and SPY, QQQ, VOO, SMH, and XLF also standing out ([ETF.com](https://www.etf.com/sections/daily-etf-flows/sp-500-etfs-flows-surge-ark-leads-outflows)). iShares’ Q1 flow review showed energy became the top equity sector by flows as Middle East conflict slowed equity momentum, while fixed income dominated late in the quarter as volatility triggered flight-to-safety flows ([iShares](https://www.ishares.com/us/insights/inside-the-market/2026-etf-market-trends-and-flows)).

Read: higher-quality institutions appear to be **barbelling**. They are adding core index/AI beta where earnings validate the story, while maintaining exposure to energy, gold, and quality fixed income as inflation/geopolitical hedges. This is not panic de-risking. It is selective accumulation with macro hedges.

## Forward-Looking Macro Risks And Opportunities

- Bullish risk: labor data cools without breaking, oil stabilizes, and AI earnings continue to validate capex. Path: lower yields -> stable DXY -> QQQ/SMH leadership -> BTC breakout -> gold consolidates but stays supported.
- Bearish risk: oil re-accelerates or Hormuz guidance fails. Path: inflation premium rises -> 2Y/10Y yields rise -> DXY/yen volatility tightens liquidity -> high-multiple equities and small caps underperform.
- Growth-scare risk: payrolls miss sharply. Path: long-end yields fall -> defensives/Treasuries/gold outperform -> cyclicals and credit weaken -> equities become narrower.

## Market Positioning And Sentiment

Sentiment is constructive but not complacently clean. Core equity ETF inflows and record-high index behavior show beta demand, but energy shock, yen intervention, and central-bank hawkish optionality keep the tape fragile. The market is positioned for earnings resilience, not for a renewed inflation shock. Confidence: **medium**.
