## Detailed Summary

The latest 24-hour crypto signal is mixed, not outright bearish. On March 31, 2026, Barron's reported Bitcoin down 2% to about $66,280 and Ethereum down 2.2%, which means both lagged the large relief rally in U.S. equities tied to hopes of Iran-war de-escalation. That matters because on March 30 BTC had been acting more like a hedge; on March 31 it reverted closer to a risk asset. Source: [Barron's](https://www.barrons.com/articles/bitcoin-price-xrp-strategy-cryptos-4b2d14e5), [Barron's March 30 note](https://www.barrons.com/articles/bitcoin-price-xrp-ethereum-cryptos-ffbcc0ef).

The most important fresh fundamental information in-window is that ETF flow did not break. Farside's live tables show U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs took in +$69.4 million on March 30 and a partial +$1.1 million on March 31, while U.S. spot Ethereum ETFs took in +$5.0 million on March 30 and a partial +$2.6 million on March 31. Those are not huge prints, but they matter because they indicate no panic withdrawal despite another headline-heavy macro session. Sources: [Farside BTC](https://farside.co.uk/btc/), [Farside ETH](https://farside.co.uk/eth/).

My judgment for the next 24 hours to 3 sessions is that BTC still has the cleaner tactical setup than ETH, but the edge is smaller than it was a day ago. BTC is holding institutional support through ETF flows, yet it did not confirm the March 31 equity relief. ETH has a similar problem plus a softer medium-term flow profile, although its U.S. ETF tape was at least positive in the latest partial prints. The next real driver is U.S. macro: ISM manufacturing on April 1 and payrolls on April 3 will decide whether yields and the dollar move in a way that helps crypto regain relative strength.
