## US Macro Environment

The U.S. macro mix remains awkward for the Fed. Headline inflation has clearly absorbed the oil shock, but core inflation has not yet broken high enough to force an immediate hawkish re-pricing. At the same time, collapsing consumer sentiment and higher inflation expectations tell you households are already feeling damage. That combination argues for Fed patience rather than quick easing. Sources: [BLS CPI](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/cpi.nr0.htm), [Federal Reserve FOMC minutes](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomcminutes20260318.htm), [WSJ](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/cpi-inflation-data-stock-market-04-10-2026/card/consumer-sentiment-hits-lowest-level-on-record-per-michigan-survey-5VJGLYnCPo7KERNfwiyI).

The market is still leaning toward the optimistic interpretation: if crude keeps falling, headline inflation may cool again before it gets embedded more deeply. That read is plausible, but it is not yet confirmed by the consumer data or by policy rhetoric.

This does not look like panic and it does not look like blind euphoria. It looks like selective accumulation by higher-quality capital with hedge demand still attached.

## Forward-Looking Macro Risks And Opportunities

- `Base case`: talks remain imperfect but keep crude contained; Nasdaq, BTC, and gold all stay resilient for different reasons.
- `Bull case`: diplomacy improves more than expected, crude keeps falling, DXY stays soft, and breadth finally expands.

The most important falsifier is simple: if crude rises again while the dollar firms, the whole "temporary oil shock" interpretation weakens quickly.
