# Daily Trading Insights - 2026-05-11

> [!abstract] Report Scope

## Detailed Summary

The market enters Monday with a conflicted but tradeable setup. The latest confirmed U.S. macro surprise was stronger labor: April payrolls rose 115,000 and unemployment stayed at 4.3% ([BLS](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/empsit_05082026.htm)). That supports earnings and soft-landing narratives, but it also keeps Fed easing distant, especially after the April 29 FOMC statement held rates at 3.50%-3.75% and flagged elevated inflation tied partly to global energy prices ([Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm)).

The top trading problem for 2026-05-11 to 2026-05-14 is whether the oil shock becomes an inflation shock. U.S. stock futures softened as oil surged after the U.S.-Iran deadlock, while markets looked ahead to CPI, PPI, retail sales, and Treasury auctions ([MarketWatch](https://www.marketwatch.com/story/u-s-stock-futures-fall-oil-surges-as-trump-calls-irans-latest-offer-to-end-war-totally-unacceptable-187e3d87)). The best bullish setups are in AI infrastructure, quality mega-cap platforms, nuclear/power demand, and BTC if $80,000 holds. The best hedge setups are energy, dollar/yield beneficiaries, and reduced exposure to crowded duration trades if CPI is hot.
