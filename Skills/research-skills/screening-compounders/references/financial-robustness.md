# Financial Robustness

Require at least 4 passed checks out of 5.

## 1. Net cash or near-zero net debt

Preferred:
- `Cash and equivalents > interest-bearing debt`

Acceptable edge case:
- leverage is minimal and clearly serviceable even under a weak year

Record:
- cash
- total debt
- net cash / net debt
- source period

## 2. Strong, persistently positive free cash flow

Check:
- free cash flow is positive in most or all years
- cumulative multi-year free cash flow is meaningfully positive
- free cash flow does not rely on one-off working-capital release

Useful outputs:
- `FCF margin`
- `FCF / EBIT`
- `FCF positive years / total years`

## 3. Customer and supplier diversification

Pass if filings indicate:
- no single customer above `15%` of revenue, and
- no critical supplier concentration that threatens bargaining power or resilience

If exact percentages are unavailable:
- use disclosed language from risk factors, segment notes, or major-customer notes
- label the result `qualitative pass` or `qualitative fail`

## 4. Diversified business mix

Pass if the company is not dangerously dependent on one fragile revenue pillar.

Look at:
- product concentration
- geography concentration
- end-market concentration
- channel concentration

Concentration alone is not an automatic fail if the moat is still obvious, but it weakens the robustness score.

## 5. Favorable cash conversion cycle

Prefer:
- short inventory days
- efficient receivables collection
- payable structure that reduces cash strain

For service or platform businesses:
- explain why the working-capital model is structurally favorable even if inventory is not relevant

## Output Standard

Create a table with:
- check
- metric / evidence
- result
- source
- commentary
