# Narrative Intelligence Framework

Use this reference when recent sector topics, market narratives, or catalyst interpretation matter to the shortlist.

## What To Look For

- Recent earnings beats or misses that changed the debate
- Guidance raises, cuts, or resets
- Product launches, pricing actions, or capacity changes
- Partnership announcements, customer wins, or losses
- Regulatory, legal, trade, or policy developments
- Investor days, keynotes, or conference commentary
- Analyst estimate revisions, target changes, and rating shifts

## How To Interpret

1. Identify the actual event.
2. Identify the market narrative attached to the event.
3. Check whether filings or primary disclosures support that narrative.
4. Decide whether the narrative is:
  - supported
  - overstated
  - still speculative
5. State whether the narrative should change the ranking.

## Output Rule

- Do not list headlines only.
- Explain what happened, why the market cares, and whether the reaction looks justified.
- If the topic is still unfolding, say what evidence would resolve it.
