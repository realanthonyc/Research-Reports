## Detailed Summary

The cleanest read for the last 24 hours is that macro and flows, not protocol news, still controlled BTC and ETH. The two biggest tradable facts were: first, Iran/Hormuz headlines pushed risk appetite around all day on April 7; second, U.S.-listed spot Bitcoin ETFs printed a much stronger inflow for April 6, which helped BTC hold up better than a pure risk proxy should have. Fortune/Bloomberg-reported market coverage said spot Bitcoin ETFs drew $471.3 million on April 6, the strongest daily inflow since late February, even as Bitcoin slipped back below $69,000 on April 7 when traders refocused on the Iran deadline. Sources: [Fortune on Bitcoin and ETF inflows](https://fortune.com/2026/04/07/bitcoin-slides-with-risk-assets-as-trumps-iran-ultimatum-looms/), [Investing crypto market report](https://www.investing.com/news/cryptocurrency-news/bitcoin-price-today-dips-below-69k-as-trumps-iran-threat-dents-risk-appetite-4599715).

That combination matters because it separates `headline volatility` from `underlying sponsorship`. If BTC were trading purely as a high-beta risk asset, the Iran deadline plus a firm dollar should have produced a cleaner downside break. Instead, the asset stayed in a wide but recognizable range, while ETF inflows improved sharply. The better interpretation is that macro is still capping upside, but institutional demand is no longer leaning aggressively the other way. Ether did not enjoy the same level of confirmed support. Fortune's 9:00 a.m. ET price check showed ETH at $2,079.89, down 2.89% from the prior morning, and Farside's most recent published U.S. spot Ethereum ETF data for April 6 showed a net inflow of $120.2 million, but the broader market still treated ETH as the weaker macro beta during the April 7 risk wobble. Sources: [Fortune Ethereum price](https://fortune.com/article/price-of-ethereum-04-07-2026/), [Farside ETH flows](https://farside.co.uk/ethereum-etf-flow-all-data/).

The macro transmission path is unchanged. Reuters reported Brent near $110.19 and the dollar near recent highs on April 7 ahead of Trump's Iran deadline, while Barron's yield coverage showed the 2-year Treasury yield near 3.85%-3.87% and the 10-year around 4.33%-4.35%. That remains a mildly restrictive setup for both BTC and ETH. It does not force liquidation, but it does keep crypto dependent on either a softer dollar, easing oil, or a separate regulatory/flow catalyst. Sources: [Reuters on stocks and oil](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/stocks-struggle-oil-jumps-as-trumps-iran-deadline-looms-4599591), [Reuters on the dollar](https://www.investing.com/news/forex-news/dollar-kept-aloft-as-another-trump-deadline-looms-4599571), [Barron's yields recap](https://www.barrons.com/livecoverage/stock-market-news-today-040726/card/u-s-treasury-yields-stabilize-in-fragile-sentiment-TmxoQEN8Je8ZnA59qNNb).

My judgment for the next 24 hours to 3 sessions is that BTC still has the better setup than ETH because it now has both a macro-alternative narrative and fresh ETF demand evidence. ETH can bounce if risk appetite improves, but it still looks more reactive than leading. The best bullish path is: ceasefire holds -> oil eases -> DXY fades -> BTC re-tests the $70,000 area and ETH catches up more slowly. The best bearish path is: ceasefire fails or oil re-extends -> dollar stays firm -> BTC holds relatively better but ETH underperforms again.
