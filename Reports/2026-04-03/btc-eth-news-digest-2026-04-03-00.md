## Detailed Summary

The newest BTC/ETH signal is still primarily macro, not crypto-native. On April 2, crypto sold off with the rest of global risk after Trump's harder Iran rhetoric kept oil elevated, but the more important read is that BTC and ETH both failed the immediate safe-haven test. Yahoo Finance said Bitcoin opened at $68,097.27 and fell to about $66,172 by 8:10 a.m. EDT, while Ethereum opened at $2,139.63 and fell to about $2,030 over the same window. Source: [Yahoo Finance crypto prices](https://finance.yahoo.com/personal-finance/investing/article/bitcoin-and-ethereum-price-today-thursday-april-2-2026-prices-lose-ground-after-trump-promises-quick-fierce-end-to-war-120045450.html).

That matters because the macro backdrop is still split. Reuters reported initial jobless claims fell to 202,000 on April 2, while Treasury yields ultimately finished below their intraday highs, with the 10-year at 4.31% and the 2-year at 3.79%. That keeps alive the possibility that softer-growth pricing can reassert itself after Friday payrolls. But oil is still the spoiler. AP said U.S. crude reached $111.54 and Brent $109.03, which keeps the inflation-and-dollar channel alive and limits how aggressively traders can lean long crypto into the weekend. Sources: [Reuters on claims](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/articles/us-weekly-jobless-claims-fall-123751021.html), [Treasury snapshot](https://files.advisorperspectives.com/dshort/updates/2026/04/02/treasury-yields-snapshot-april-2-2026), [AP market wrap](https://apnews.com/article/6fc90a2e50b1252cde130fc3e0ce0da3).

The most actionable crypto-specific evidence is in ETF flows, and the latest flow tables are not bullish. Farside showed April 1 net outflows of $173.7 million from U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs and $7.1 million from U.S. spot Ethereum ETFs. That is not a disorderly liquidation, but it does mean institutional money was not stepping in aggressively on the latest macro wobble. Sources: [Farside BTC](https://farside.co.uk/btc/), [Farside ETH](https://farside.co.uk/eth/).

My judgment for the next 24 hours to 3 sessions is that BTC still has the cleaner setup than ETH, but only conditionally. If payrolls on April 3 are soft enough to push yields and DXY lower, BTC can recover first because it needs less of a pure risk-on backdrop than ETH. If payrolls are firm, oil stays high, and DXY lifts, both assets can remain heavy, with ETH more vulnerable because its demand base is thinner and its ETF flow picture is weaker in absolute dollar terms.
