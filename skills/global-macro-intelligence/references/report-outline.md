# Global Macro Report Outline

Use this structure unless the user requests a shorter or differently formatted note.

## Detailed Summary
- Summarize the dominant macro regime.
- State whether the backdrop is risk-on or risk-off, inflationary or disinflationary, and liquidity-expanding or contracting.
- Explain the core transmission chain in slower, clearer language.
- State the bottom-line cross-asset implication and your explicit judgment.

## Brief Summary
- Provide a fast-read version of the regime, the key driver, the main judgment, and the next market-moving risk.

## Major Recent Developments
- Cover the most important just-happened, recent, and upcoming developments.
- Prioritize central-bank communication, inflation and labor data, fiscal announcements, geopolitical shocks, and major commodity moves.
- Explain why each event matters for pricing, not just the headline.
- Explicitly note market expectation, actual result, and reaction where relevant.

## US Macro Environment
- Analyze growth, inflation, employment, financial conditions, and fiscal backdrop.
- Explain how the Fed is likely to interpret the mix.
- Lay out the most plausible policy paths and what would shift them.

## Global Macro Environment
- China: growth pulse, policy support, property stress, credit, yuan, and commodity demand.
- Japan: BOJ stance, yen, JGBs, and global liquidity spillovers.
- Europe: growth, ECB stance, energy or fiscal issues, and euro implications.
- Add geopolitics where relevant to supply chains, trade, or energy.

## Interest Rates And Liquidity
- Discuss `2Y`, `10Y`, curve shape, real yields, term premium if relevant, and financial conditions.
- Include Fed balance-sheet dynamics, reserve/liquidity context, and any meaningful tightening or easing signals.
- Explain what rates/liquidity imply for duration-sensitive and speculative assets.
- Strengthen the liquidity read by distinguishing:
  - policy stance (`Fed path`, `QT/QE`, central-bank balance-sheet direction)
  - system liquidity (`bank reserves`, `RRP`, `TGA`, collateral/funding conditions)
  - market liquidity (`credit availability`, spread behavior, risk-taking capacity)
- Explain whether the market is reacting to actual liquidity injection, reduced liquidity drain, or simply a change in rate expectations.

## Dollar And FX Dynamics
- Analyze `DXY` and the main crosses that matter for the current regime.
- Emphasize rate differentials, safe-haven demand, capital flows, and relative growth.
- Explain the implications for commodities, EM risk, and global liquidity.

## Commodities
- Cover gold, silver, copper, and oil.
- Explain each through growth expectations, real yields, inflation hedging, dollar direction, and geopolitical supply risks.
- For gold, explicitly analyze the balance between real yields, dollar moves, central-bank demand, reserve diversification, geopolitical hedging, and recession/financial-stability demand.
- If gold strength appears inconsistent with higher real yields or a firmer dollar, identify the offsetting force instead of leaving the move unexplained.

## Crypto Market Context
- Explain how macro conditions shape Bitcoin and broader crypto.
- Link crypto to liquidity, real yields, dollar direction, ETF flows if relevant, and risk appetite.

## Cross-Asset Macro Interpretation
- Synthesize the note into one coherent regime framework.
- Explain how equities, rates, FX, commodities, credit, and crypto are confirming or contradicting each other.

## Forward-Looking Macro Risks And Opportunities
- Identify the events or shocks most likely to change the regime.
- For each, explain the probable transmission path through yields, dollar, liquidity, and risk assets.

## Key Indicators To Monitor
- Include upcoming inflation, labor, activity, central-bank, yield, dollar, credit, commodity, and liquidity indicators.
- State why each one matters and what signal would be bullish, bearish, or regime-changing.

## Market Positioning And Sentiment
- Assess volatility, options structure, ETF and fund flows, futures positioning, and any crowding indicators that matter.
- Explain how positioning could amplify or mute the macro thesis.
