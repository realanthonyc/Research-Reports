Sources: [WSJ on Bitcoin](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/stock-market-today-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-04-17-2026/card/bitcoin-gains-after-iran-foreign-minister-declares-strait-of-hormuz-open-OmNI2Wf8tld0CaPJtZUO), [Farside BTC ETF flows](https://farside.co.uk/btc/), [Farside ETH ETF flows](https://farside.co.uk/eth/).

That tells the same story as the ETF data: both assets are rising, but BTC still has the stronger sponsorship and cleaner macro role. Sources: [Fortune on ETH, April 17](https://fortune.com/article/price-of-ethereum-04-17-2026/), [Fortune on BTC, April 17](https://fortune.com/article/price-of-bitcoin-04-17-2026/).

The macro constraint has not disappeared. March CPI on April 10 was `0.9% m/m` and `3.3% y/y`; March PPI on April 14 was `0.5% m/m` and `4.0% y/y`. Crypto is rallying because the market thinks the energy shock may reverse before those headline pressures become more persistent. If crude rebounds or DXY snaps back, the bullish crypto read weakens quickly. Sources: [BLS CPI](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_04102026.htm), [BLS PPI](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ppi.nr0.htm).

My judgment is that BTC has the cleaner `intraday to 1-3 sessions` setup, while ETH is still more likely to follow than lead unless the risk-on tape broadens further or ETH-specific flows improve materially.
